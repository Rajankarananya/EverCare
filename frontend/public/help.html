Frequently Asked Questions

Medical Questions

What's the difference between Alzheimer's and dementia?

Dementia is an umbrella term for symptoms affecting memory and thinking. Alzheimer's is the most common type of dementia, accounting for 60-80% of cases, and involves specific brain changes.

Are there treatments to slow progression?

Current medications (cholinesterase inhibitors and memantine) may temporarily improve symptoms. Lifestyle interventions like diet, exercise, and cognitive stimulation may also help slow progression.

Caregiving Challenges

How do I handle repetitive questions?

Stay patient, provide simple answers, and redirect to activities. Write down answers for reference. Avoid saying "You already asked that."

What if my loved one refuses care?

Approach calmly, break tasks into steps, offer choices when possible, and try again later if resistance continues. Consult their doctor about possible medical causes.

Legal & Financial Planning

When should we establish power of attorney?

As early as possible after diagnosis while the person can still participate in decisions. Requires legal documentation and may vary by state.

What financial assistance is available?

Medicare covers some costs; Medicaid may help later. Veterans benefits, long-term care insurance, and local Area Agencies on Aging offer resources.
